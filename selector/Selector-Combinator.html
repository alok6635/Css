Combinators-

combinator is explains the relationship between the selectors

  • descendant selector (space)
  • child selector (>)
  • adjacent sibling selector (+)
  • general sibling selector (~)

Descendant Selector -

descendant selector matches all elements that are descendants of a specified element.

Ex- -

Paragraph 1 in the div.

Paragraph 2 in the div.

Paragraph 3 Not in a div.

Child Selector (>) -

child selector selects all elements that are the children of a specified element.

Ex- -

Paragraph 1 in the div.

Paragraph 2 in the div.

Paragraph 3 in the div.

Paragraph 4 Not in a div.

Adjacent Sibling Selector (+) -

+ selector is used to select an element that is directly after another specific element.

Ex- -

Paragraph 1 in the div.

Paragraph 2 After a div.

Paragraph 3 After a div.

Paragraph 4 in the div.

Paragraph 5 After a div.

General Sibling Selector (~) -

sibling selector (~) selects all elements that are next siblings of a specified element.

Ex -

Paragraph 1.

Paragraph 2.

Paragraph 3.
